About Me

My name is Gabe Harms and I am currently attending Pennyslvannia State University as a senior. I was born in Santa Barbara, CA in June of 1993. I spent most of my childhood in southern Califiornia, living right outside of Los Angelos. At the age of about 10 my family moved to central Pennsylvania in order to be closer to family. During my teenage years I attended State College Area High School. Upon graduation of high school I started my college career at Penn State majoring in Computer Engineering.

My interest in computer was sparked during high school when I found out how to fix several game consoles including PS3s and XBOXs. Ever since I first opened up a PS3 I have been completly facinated with technology. It led to a curiosity that drove me to want to learn as much as I possibly could about computers. As a result I took my first programming and electrical class during high school. Craving more, I enrolled in Penn State's Computer Engineering program.

I have thoroughly enjoyed my time at Penn State learning a wide range of subjects within computer science and electrical engineering. At the end of my sophmore year, I took an internship in Salt Lake City, UT with Northrop Grumman. During my time there, I was a Systems Engineer looking for safety concerns with autonomous air vehicles. At the end of the summer, I was asked to work part time during my fall semester at Penn State. Thus, I worked remotely during school, putting in about 20 hours per week. At the end of the fall semester I was offered another internship with a company local to state college called Videon Central Inc. Thus I started in the spring semester of my junior year as a hardware engineer. I worked again 20 hours per week, and my tasks ranged from schematic design to testing. I worked through the summer and was again asked to continue working part time during the fall of my senior year 20 hours a week.

My experiences, both academic and professional, have given me a very diversified background. I had the opportunity to gain experience in hardware design at Videon Central, and have even passed my Electrical and Computer Engineering FE exam. However, I also thoroughly enjoy designing software though. I have started many of my own projects in which I push myself to learn different languages and types of programming. I feel competent in both object oriented and structural programming. Thus, now I am currently looking for full time positions in all types of fields, hardware or software.
